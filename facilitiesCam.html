If at any point of your student life, food hasn’t been the only thing that made you happy, excuse me but were you even a student? In that regard, as in many more, a campus that understands is all one needs. For students at IITG, there are 99 problems and food is not one of them. We are a straightforward lot which knows that the mess is frankly a mess, and we are are firm believers of the right to choose what we ingest. And not without reason. You can be picky without feeling bad about about it, because from Hindustan ki Jaan chai-samose to that dainty glass of chilled vanilla frappé, everything is within your reach (as soon as you figure out how to stop being broke all the time). Allow yourself the pleasure as I take you through a brief guide to how gastronomy works here at IITG.

Food Court

You know the New Students’ Activity Center is a genius place to put a boatload of eating joints; especially since it’s in honour of the most popular activity the students actually do. For gentler readers, yes, I am talking about eating. It has the following to offer:

Subway:
Does this need explanation? Well, aside from having all the latest releases, this is the place for them who like it a little extra. A bit of extra mayo, a couple more olives- name it, have it.

Foodie’s World:
The favourite fast food place of the FC, they are deliciously notorious for adding their own twists to classics like pizza, fried chicken and doing things to Maggi which were previously considered sacrilege. Also, you might have four grandkids by the time your food arrives.

Manikanta Restaurant:
The hub of all food south Indian, enthusiasts often forget they sell anything besides the gift-to-humanity chicken biryani. On rarer occasions, the chutney served with the dosa is likened to cement, but that’s a matter for another time. Also, if you’re thinking of sambhar vada, *personal views alert* abort immediate.

Florentine:
This is your token air-conditioned multi-cuisine, if you’re looking for The Great North Indian Gravy dishes, or if you don’t mind bones in your schezwan chicken. According to sources, anyone studying for less than a PhD is officially too poor for it.

Ambrosia:
This is for my buddies with a sweet tooth. Ambrosia the official darling is here to help with pastries, brownies, lava cakes, an unending supply of soft drinks and the carrot cake which nobody buys. When Baskin Robbins gets a bit too hard on our poor wallets, this place always ensures you never go without dessert. Speaking of which.

Baskin Robbins:
When the going gets tough, the tough get going Baskin Robbins. Not like you’ll make through it if you’re not tough enough. Many bravehearts have tried and failed to make a satisfactory choice in under ten minutes. The best course of action, as of now, seems to be taking small bits of all 9999 flavours in the name of ‘tasting’ and running away before the shop guy finds out.

CAFE COFFEE DAY

More than the usual caffeinated delights, CCD upholds its status as the restaurant with the best view. Sitting on the semi open air balcony in front of the library, you can enjoy a full panoramic view of the best part of the academic complex, and watch the sky change colours as you pick your roasted coffee beans off the whipped cream.

VINAIGRETTE

None is unaware of the fact that the unholy pangs of hunger can, in fact, erupt at any moment. What are puny hurdles like time when a determined student wants a snack at midnight? Fear not, for the Vinaigrette 24-hour canteen is at your service. It serves arguably the most wholesome, delicious and affordable food in the campus, and what’s better- at any time you please. It is also situated at generous distances from all hostels, but that would only make sure you’re hungry enough.

Silver Dine

If there is anything that every sad soul on campus wants at the same time, it is to make sure that nobody that qualifies as a senior should go un-looted. This is the one place which’ll make you enjoy it in inverse proportionality with the time you’ve spent here. Even though you’ll experience momentary wishes of non-existence when you have to pay for for the exquisite bill, as long as its seniors, fine-dine and fun, everything is okay, right?

Khokha

Speaking of on-campus food joints, 300 metres off campus should be legal, right? Situated right outside the faculty gate, Khokha=fun evenings. The frothing creamy tea here one can kill for, not to mention the delish plates of chicken pakora, or samosa chaat. It is the one stop de-stress to all your distress (haha get it? No? Nevermind.
