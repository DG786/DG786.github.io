ABOUT CHEMISTRY

Department of Chemistry, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati is a globally recognized, department in science and technology with a growing list of accolades earned nationally and internationally. It offers the 4 year bachelor's’ programme - Chemical Science and Technology (a.k.a CST).

COURSE STRUCTURE y

The structure of the undergraduate CST course consists of a package of standard undergraduate Chemistry courses, designed to give a rudimentary understanding of most of the basic and popular areas of research to the students. Studies deal with Quantum Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Environmental, Physical, Organic, Chemical Kinetics, Computational Chemistry, etc. However this being an engineering course rather than one on theoretical chemistry , the traditional Chemistry courses are bundled along with relevant courses from other disciplines such as those in Mechanical Engineering like Heat and Mass Transfer, Fluids, etc. Along with this, students can take electives from the Chemistry department and other departments from the sixth semester to follow any particular interests one might have.

FUTURE PROSPECTS

Opportunities for students after graduation are varied. A doctorate degree is a necessity if the students want to be researchers. IIT Guwahati undergraduates have joined the top schools in US and Europe. Recent graduates have made it to some of the best research groups in Europe. It is also common for students to get graduate positions at top Indian research institutes. Many students have been known to go for management and getting admission into top IIMs and ISB. In addition, some students go for higher education in other fields such as UPSC. The placement records are better than most other branches in IITG with students getting placed in many Analytics, Finance, Software development in companies such as Goldman Sachs, American Express, Futures First, ZS Associates, EXL, CGI, Wipro to name a few. Many have also been placed in management positions in various companies. There is abundance of opportunity for those who work hard.

FACT: “CST is recognised as the ‘chillest’ course on campus and this recognition is given weight to by the large number of CST undergrads participating in various extracurricular activities.”
